REALLOCATING MY CANDY BAR BUDGET

by Jess Goodwin, date

     

It took me awhile to decide what I was going to write about for this month’s “self improvement” theme, because virtually every area of my life and personality could use improving: finances, diet, the discipline to actually write that YA novel, the ability to communicate in a sincere manner, etc.

I settled on my finances because, at the age of 28, it’s something that weighs upon my mind like a bag of bricks, each labeled with names like “student loan debt,” “credit card debt” and “candy bar budget.” I’m terrible with money (something I’ve written about on Femsplain before), and as I inch closer to the day my fiscally responsible boyfriend and I move in together, all I can think is, Shit. I don’t want my money issues to fuck up my relationship.

I know myself, though, and it’s not enough to simply write about how I’m going to start budgeting and, like, totally reinvent the way I buy stuff (or rather, don’t buy stuff). I need motivation, and what better motivation than to say on the Internet -- where nothing you say can ever be taken back -- that I’m going to essentially keep a spending diary for the month of July?

Every day, for the next 30 days, I’ll offer up a small update about how the whole refinancing-my-life-thing is going. Will I resist the temptation to hit up Forever 21 and buy myself that totally necessary T-shirt with the donut print? Will I cave and order a $20 Seamless meal when I could have just walked a block up the street and gotten a 25-cent package of ramen? (I don’t expect my diet to improve during July.) Will I finally go through all my shit and figure out what’s worth selling and then use the money I make to make a dent in my debt, or will I finally go through all my shit and figure out what’s worth selling and then use the money I make to treat myself to an acupuncture session? Only time will tell! Anything could happen! Hold on for dear life!

Also, feel free to root me on when I save money and chastise me when I fall off the wagon -- I'll need all the help I can get -- and share your own tales of and hopes for self-improvement. Tell us all about the time you reached your ideal BMI, or how you want to start volunteering, or even how you feel like you’re absolutely perfect just the way you are. We’ll root you on right back.

Day 1

I did not resist the temptation to order a burger on Seamless (which I still need to Venmo Gabi for lol), and also forgot I’d made plans to take my roommate out for dinner for her birthday. Add that onto the breakfast, birth control and MetroCard I bought and after just one day I have $63.11 less to show for my efforts. I’m off to a great start!

Day 2

My second day of the financial cleanse went much better. I spent $8.48, on lunch and a box of cat food, then $1.25 on a Capri Sun and a tiny bag of Oreos. (I don’t really count the $1.25 because I paid with dimes and nickels and that’s not real money right?) The problem now is feel I should reward myself for spending less than $10 in a day, and I don’t know how to reward myself without spending money.

Day 3

I lasted until 6 p.m. without spending any money yesterday, thanks to a bag of slightly stale, off-brand cereal I found sitting atop my fridge in the morning. (Score!)

During a visit to Key Food that evening, my roommate quickly discovered the store was closing for construction and everything was 25%, and all hell broke loose. She ran around with barely contained glee, while I made a beeline for the cheese I’d originally and wistfully abandoned.

I tried to make smart purchases that will last at least until next weekend (maybe even beyond!), and think I did okay:

Chicken breast
Boxed mashed potatoes
Sausage links
Grapes
Grape tomatoes
Those tiny mozzarella cheese balls (to eat with the grape tomatoes)
Goat cheese
Sliced American cheese (YES I LIKE CHEESE OKAY)
Sliced turkey and chicken (for sammiches)
Pudding!
A 2-liter of Pepsi

Overall, it came to $28.88. I could’ve done without the goat cheese, the most extravagant item on, but it’s goat cheese, and I can put it on the sammiches. The pudding was also unnecessary, but I haven’t had pudding in like, two years, and I’m only human.

Day 4

Yeah, so, I went back to Key Foods yesterday. With a cart. And a backpack. Everything was 25% off! Plus there were all these other random sales going on. I can’t in good conscience pass up buying 20 pouches of Capri Sun for $4 -- that’s 20 cents a pouch, as opposed to the 50-75 cents I usually spend per pouch at local delis. IT WAS A STEAL.

I ended up buying about $42 worth of food (which would have been like $66 before all the savings), and got a variety of things that will last awhile, such as:

Pasta
Ramen
Soup
Raviolis
Macaroni and cheese
A giant box of cereal and a giant bag of cereal

I also went to a BBQ in the afternoon (Happy belated Fourth, btw) and brought this INSANE Entenmann’s marshmallow cake that ended up being about $6. All in all I spent less than $50 yesterday, which brings the tally up to $149 after… four days.

I might need some help.

Day 5

I went to the movies yesterday (using my MoviePass, a Netflix-esque movie subscription, which was technically -- technically -- already paid for in June) and did not buy any of those little corn dog nuggets, even though I really really wanted to. (How strong am I?) Also, my man bought me breakfast, so the only money I spent was $10 on a MetroCard. UP TOP.

Day 6

Made my breakfast, made my lunch, made my dinner -- all I bought was a loaf of bread, box of Thin Wheats (which are cheaper than Wheat Thins, heyo) and a large glass bottle from the dollar section of Target (because duh, I can put things in it), all for the low low total of $4.67. I hope you’re all air high-fiving me.

Day 7

I got really excited because I thought I was going to be able to write, "I SPENT $0 YESTERDAY" but then I remembered I had to put money on my MetroCard. Damn this city and its overpriced public transportation. DAMN IT.

Day 8

I thought this was finally going to be the day I didn’t spend any money, simply because I had no money, but then I found $4 in my pants pocket, so I bought some Ho Hos

Day 9

Boyfriend bought me dinner last night, so finally -- FINALLY -- I didn’t spend a cent yesterday.

However, now is the hard part, because plot twist -- part of the reason I’ve been able to refrain from buying shit for the past few days (apart from subway rides, because DAMN THIS CITY) is simply because I had like no money.

But I got paid today, so now I have to resist buying useless crap not because I literally can’t, but because it’s useless crap. *prayer hands emoji*

Days 10, 11 and 12

Just got back from my WEEKEND SPENDING BENDER. Jk, I was in Pennsylvania for my boyfriend’s family reunion, and because it was Pennsylvania, there wasn’t too much for me to spend money on.

On Friday my bank account took a pretty big hit, BUT it was a responsible hit, because I paid a bunch of bills and refilled my MetroCard (AGAIN).

As for things I didn’t need to spend money on? The total came to about $55, mainly because virtually all my food and train tickets were taken care of.

Later today I’m going to talk to Katie Krumpter, a financial advisor and occasional Femsplain contributor, to see if she has some tips for how to keep my spending down. *fingers crossed*

Days 13, 14 and 15

Man it’s hard to update something every day (apart from Twitter obvs). I’ve still been doing okay money-wise, though on Tuesday I did spend an alarming $16 for an umbrella so I wouldn’t get caught in the sudden rainstorm that ended approximately three seconds before I went back outside.

I’m currently on a mini-vacation in Illinois (I’ll be in Chicago starting Friday) and while I haven’t spent any money while actually in Illinois, I spent like $25 just at the airport, because it’s the airport and everything’s movie theater expensive.

At the suggestion of financial advisor and occasional Femsplain contributor Katie Krumpter, I signed up for Mint. (Or rather, I re-signed up for Mint, since I used it once before but got too lazy to bother with it. Sensing a theme…?)

Mint is fun, because, at a glance, I can see how often I ordered and how much I spent on Seamless in a given month. For example, in May, I ordered Seamless 19 -- NINETEEN -- times, sometimes twice in one day, because I’m a monster.

Katie and I also discussed my complete lack of impulse control, and while she kindly told me not to beat myself up too much, I’m pretty sure I actually need to beat myself up when it comes to money, because part of the reason I’m not careful with it is my tendency to rationalize buying things no one needs.

Days 16-26

Yeah so keeping a diary is hard. Kudos to anyone who actually manages to write in a diary or journal every day.

Anyway… Chicago kind of tapped me out, because there’s a lot of food there and I like to eat. Also, there was this bar with these shots that taste like oatmeal cookies and, well, you know how it goes.

Also, the end of the month is generally when my bills are due, and there was an issue with a loan from my grandparents. Specifically, I kind of put off paying them back in a timely fashion and they’ve finally come to collect and, in the interest of transparency, I had to ask for an advance on my next paycheck at one of my freelancing gigs. (There were tears, as per usual.)

Since being back in New York I’ve been able to pretty much avoid spending money (except on MetroCard fares, because DAMN THE MTA), but I still have about five days to do something stupid financially, so stay tuned.
